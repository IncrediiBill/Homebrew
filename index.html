Mehdian AasimarDwarfElfGnomeHalf-ElfHalf-OrcHalflingHuman The Mehdian Republic is a prominent nation on Geoctum, transitioning from a tyrannical human-only power to a beacon of reconciliation and stability. Following the death of the Mad King, a new capital named Valoria was established, promoting internal development and trade while facing internal threats from loyalists seeking to restore the old regime. The Council of Regents, comprising leaders focused on military, cultural affairs, and the common people's welfare, aims to rebuild trust and foster peace, despite ongoing challenges with neighboring nations and internal factions. The Mehdian faith, centered around the Radiant Mother, emphasizes community and hope, influencing daily life and governance.
The Oldstone Alliance AasimarDwarfElfGnomeGoliathHalf-ElfHalf-OrcHalflingHumanKytinnSs'kaal The Oldstone Alliance is a continent-spanning militia that protects independent towns and villages in Geoctum. Founded by Captain Oldstone after the traumatic event known as the Pull, it promotes self-reliance and mutual aid among mixed-race settlements. The Alliance operates with a decentralized structure led by the Warden-General from Oakhaven, supported by Regional Wardens and local Town Captains. Its primary mission is to defend against banditry and monstrous incursions while fostering a sense of shared identity among diverse communities. The Alliance maintains cautious relationships with other nations, providing stability in areas they cannot control.
AlfiyaElfHalf-Elf Alfiya is an elven nation located in the Whispering Woods, centered around the living city of Cosminas, governed by the Conclave of Elder Branches. The Conclave consists of representatives from various elven sub-races, each with unique roles and perspectives. The city features a harmonious blend of nature and magic, with interconnected living structures and bioluminescent flora. Relations with neighboring nations are cautious, particularly with Mehdian, while the Elves maintain a rich spiritual tradition centered around the Great Song, emphasizing their connection to nature and vigilance against past atrocities.
The Stoneheart Empire DwarfGnomeSvirfneblin The Stoneheart Empire is an ancient Dwarven nation allied with Gnomes, known for its hidden cities and mastery of stone and metal. Its political landscape is shaped by a Council of the Mountain, focusing on tradition and the threat from the Underdark. The Great Sundering led to the loss of their capital, Kor-Dulgar, and the council keeps its secrets about the High Thane's fate. The Empire's culture is deeply rooted in subterranean existence, with a unique pantheon of Stoneheart Gods. The military comprises three legions: Deepwardens, Hearthguard Enforcers, and Stonefist Sentinels, each with specialized roles in defense and internal security. The Ghost Legion, a rogue elite force, seeks to recover their lost history. Settlements are remarkable feats of engineering, blending functionality with artistry.
The Wilders ElfGoliathHalf-ElfHalf-OrcHalflingHuman The Wilders are a collective of diverse clans, including Wood Elves, Halflings, Half-Orcs, and Humans, who embrace a nomadic lifestyle in Geoctum's wilderness, guided by their Seer. Their decentralized politics emphasize clan loyalty, with each clan led by a Walker. The current Seer, Lifi "Star-Whisper," interprets omens and acts as a diplomat. The clans, such as the Whispering Pines and Riverbend Wanderers, each have unique skills and traditions. The Wilders maintain cautious relationships with settled nations, prioritize survival, and have a distinct spiritual view that respects nature and the divine.
GoliathsGoliath Goliaths on Geoctum are rare, independent tribes valuing strength and self-improvement, often exploring rugged landscapes. They have complex relationships with other factions, respecting the Stoneheart Empire but finding them puzzling, sharing a kinship with the Wilders, and viewing Ironhide Warbands and others as rivals. Goliaths may work as guards or guides for the Oldstone Alliance, valuing fair compensation for their strength.
VanalirTiefling Vanalir is a Tiefling nation in Geoctum, burdened by prejudice and the knowledge that their world is a collection of "Pulled" fragments. Governed by the Council of Veiled Wisdom, they prioritize self-preservation and the secrecy of their origins. Their diverse infernal features reflect their complex heritage, and they rely on resourcefulness in harsh desert conditions. The Council consists of respected scholars and geomancers, each with unique roles, overseeing the preservation of their sacred lore. Their settlements are fortified and designed for defense, while their view of deities is more scholarly than worshipful, acknowledging the powers that shaped their existence.
Dragonborn Clans Dragonborn The Dragonborn of Geoctum consist of various clans divided by allegiance to Bahamut or Tiamat, leading to an ongoing internal conflict. Bahamut clans value justice and honor, while Tiamat clans prioritize power and greed. Key clans include the Adamant Scales, Sky-Sentinel, Tide-Breaker, Sunken Hoard, and Oasis Keepers, who embody noble ideals, contrasted by the aggressive Cinder-Scourge, Shadow-Fang, Frost-Heart, Storm-Chaser, and Venom Kin clans. Smaller groups like the Wandering Scales and Cinder-Heart Mercenaries seek freedom from the war. Relations with other nations are minimal, with Dragonborn viewing outsiders as largely irrelevant to their divine struggles.
Ss’kaalSs'kaal The Ss'kaal, or Ash-Scales, are resilient reptilian humanoids adapted to the harsh Ash Wastes. They are agile, with distinct features like a forked tongue for sensing and nictitating membranes for eye protection. Living as nomadic hunter-gatherers in family groups called clutches, their culture prioritizes survival and resourcefulness, viewing other races as wasteful. Their creation myth, "The Great Scouring," describes their origins from the world's cleansing embers and dust.
Kytinn Kytinn Kytinn are small, ferret-like humanoids known for their intelligence and tinkering skills, living in underground networks called Burrows. They value resourcefulness and must undertake a Proving Run at 14 to prove their worth by acquiring valuable offerings for their Families. Kytinn society is insular, with strict laws against outsiders entering their Burrows, and they are organized into Families specializing in various trades, such as mechanics, herbalism, and espionage. Each Kytinn adopts a new name upon joining a Family, marking their transition to adulthood and societal contribution.
The Bog-Striders Bog-striders The Bog-Striders are reptilian humanoids who remember their arrival during "The Great Drowning," which shapes their identity and survival instincts. They are tall, lean, and camouflaged with scales that vary by tribe. Their culture is pragmatic, focusing on survival and animistic spirituality, with shamans as respected leaders. Major tribes include the Moss-Skin Tribe, known for diplomacy; the aggressive Black-Scale Hunters; the stealthy Reed-Stalker Tribe; and the reclusive Deep-Dwellers. The Silt-Guards maintain peace among tribes, while the Mire-Touched are outcasts who deal with malevolent hags.
Ragged Crowns BugbearsGoblinsHobgoblinsOgres The goblinoids of Geoctum, known as the Ragged Crowns, are divided into tribes led by self-proclaimed Blood Kings and Queens, each with conflicting claims to royalty. Key tribes include the Iron Legion, known for their discipline and ambition to unite the goblinoids; the Skull-Throne Kingdom, which seeks legitimacy through conquest; and the Gutter-Scourge Tribe, characterized by chaotic raiding. Other tribes like the Blighted Fang and Rust-Eater Clan showcase unique leadership and resourcefulness. Each tribe has distinct settlements and goals, reflecting their varied philosophies and traditions.
Ironhide Warbands Half-OrcOrcs The Ironhide Warbands are a disciplined, religiously zealous society of orcs, led by Warlord Shamans who interpret divine visions. They engage in brutal rituals and seek constant conflict, establishing fortified camps in rugged territories. Their spiritual beliefs emphasize reincarnation and proving worth through battle, guided by six War-Gods representing various aspects of warfare. The Ironhide Code, a universal set of principles, dictates their actions and defines their identity, enforcing honor and the pursuit of worth through combat. Various warbands, such as the Bone-Crown Chaplains and Ironbound Mercenaries, embody different aspects of their culture and beliefs, each with unique practices and appearances
Drow Drow The Drow are a cruel, matriarchal society of Dark Elves devoted to the Spider Queen, Lolth, characterized by ambition, treachery, and dark magic. Their political landscape is dominated by noble houses led by Matron Mothers, with constant power struggles intensified by a mysterious silence from Lolth. The Handmaiden, a powerful Yochlol, manipulates Drow society, masquerading as Lolth's voice while pursuing its own agenda. Drow cities like Ilelesterûn and Ruaholme are centers of dark magic and espionage, with ongoing conflicts against the Duergar and ambitions to expand into the Stoneheart Empire. The Drow view other races as potential slaves or sacrificial victims, with a focus on ritual sacrifices to maintain their power.
Duergar Duergar Duergar are the avaricious Grey Dwarves of Geoctum's Beneath, known for their ruthless, xenophobic society centered on slavery, mining, and brutal craftsmanship. Their political structure is a meritocracy ruled by fear and aggression, with frequent internal power struggles. They despise surface dwellers and maintain a deep hatred for The Stoneheart Empire, viewing the Svirfneblin as rightful slaves and engaging in constant conflict with the Drow. Duergar religion reflects their grim nature, venerating Laduguer as their patron and embodying values of greed and brutal efficiency.
Svirfneblin Svirfneblin Svirfneblin, the resourceful Deep Gnomes of Geoctum's Beneath, prioritize survival and community protection against threats like the Duergar and Drow. They maintain a cautious alliance with The Stoneheart Empire for mutual support. Their religion revolves around the Old Gods, particularly Callarduran Smoothhands, whom they believe protects them through the earth. Svirfneblin fear Mind Flayers and avoid conflict, resorting to sabotage against their oppressors. They possess hidden artifacts of their history but keep them secret to avoid discovery.
