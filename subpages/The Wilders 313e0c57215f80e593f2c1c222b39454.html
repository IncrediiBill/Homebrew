The Wilders

TextThe Wilders are a collective of diverse clans, including Wood Elves, Halflings, Half-Orcs, and Humans, who embrace a nomadic lifestyle in Geoctum's wilderness, guided by their Seer. Their decentralized politics emphasize clan loyalty, with each clan led by a Walker. The current Seer, Lifi "Star-Whisper," interprets omens and acts as a diplomat. The clans, such as the Whispering Pines and Riverbend Wanderers, each have unique skills and traditions. The Wilders maintain cautious relationships with settled nations, prioritize survival, and have a distinct spiritual view that respects nature and the divine.

The Wilders are not a "nation" in the traditional sense, but a collective of diverse clans that reject settled life, choosing instead to roam Geoctum's vast wilderness. They embody freedom, self-reliance, and an intimate connection to the land, their movements dictated by the seasons, game trails, and the wisdom of their Seer. Being a mix of Wood Elves, Halflings, Half-Orcs, and Humans, the Wilders are a testament to shared purpose overriding racial differences. Their identity is Wilder, forged in shared survival.

They are unparalleled experts in survival, tracking, hunting, and foraging. They know every game trail, every hidden spring, and every edible plant in their territories. Their navigation relies on natural signs, stars, and the feel of the wind. Their camps are temporary, designed to be quickly established and easily packed up, leaving minimal trace. They use natural materials, camouflage, and often possess unique collapsible tents or shelters.

Current Political Climate & Relations

The Wilders' politics are decentralized and highly personal, based on clan loyalty and the spiritual guidance of their Seer. Their interactions with settled peoples are cautious and often transactional. Each clan is led by a Walker, a seasoned leader who decides the clan's immediate direction, hunting grounds, and internal disputes. The Walker's authority is respected but earned through wisdom and the ability to provide for the clan. The true, overarching leader of all Wilders.

The Seer is chosen by the Walkers, a process shrouded in ritual and natural omens and, upon selection, cuts all ties to their birth clan. When a Seer encounters another clan, they must travel with that clan until they meet a new one, ensuring their impartiality and spreading their wisdom across the entire Wilder collective. The Seer makes diplomatic decisions and offers broad guidance, focusing on the well-being of all Wilders.

While united by the Seer and their nomadic lifestyle, individual clans retain unique traditions, hunting practices, and internal dynamics based on their dominant racial makeup or ancestral customs.

The Seer

The current Seer of the clans is Lifi "Star-Whisper”. She is a human, chosen for her deep connection to the land and the stars, a common trait among the Hearthfire Keepers and Sky-Gazer clan. She is a woman of indeterminate age, she appears in her late 50s, but her wisdom suggests far more, with a serene, almost ethereal presence. Her eyes are a startling, luminous silver, often seeming to gaze beyond the immediate. Her long, dark hair is adorned with small, natural talismans: polished river stones, dried leaves, and tiny, intricate bone carvings. She wears simple, well-worn robes woven from natural fibres, often in muted greens and browns, blending seamlessly with the wilderness. She carries no weapons, only a staff of living, unadorned wood. She is calm, deeply insightful, and profoundly patient. Lifi speaks in a soft, melodic voice, often in riddles or poetic metaphors that require contemplation. She possesses a deep empathy for all living things and a profound understanding of the interconnectedness of Geoctum's ecosystem. She is fiercely protective of the Wilders' freedom and their way of life, and she is utterly incorruptible by external power or wealth. She believes in the inherent wisdom of nature.

Lifi is the Seer of all Wilder clans. Her primary role is to interpret omens from the land, the pink sky, and the flickering stars, guiding the collective decisions of the Wilders. She acts as their chief diplomat when dealing with other nations, always prioritizing the freedom and well-being of her people. She is the living repository of Wilder lore and traditions, and her presence brings a profound sense of calm and purpose to whichever clan she is with. Lifi does not command, but guides. She offers counsel to the Walkers, mediates inter-clan disputes, and interprets the subtle "whispers" of Geoctum's wounded nature. She makes diplomatic overtures to other nations, such as the Mehdian or the Oldstone Alliance, only when absolutely necessary, always through proxies or at neutral sites. She is a master of wilderness survival and can move through the most untamed lands with ease.

Lifi's movement is unique and central to her impartiality. When the clan she is currently with encounters another Wilder clan, Lifi must then travel with that newly encountered clan. She will remain with this new clan until they encounter another, and so on. This ensures she experiences the diverse challenges and perspectives of all Wilder clans firsthand, preventing her from becoming too entrenched with any single group and allowing her to guide the collective effectively. Her movements are tracked by the Walkers, allowing the party to find her by inquiring with any Wilder clan.

Ten Clans of the Free Winds

The Whispering Pines Clan

Renowned for their unparalleled stealth and knowledge of ancient forest lore. They move through the trees like ghosts, their communication often a series of subtle calls and gestures. They are master archers and trackers, and their shamans are deeply attuned to the oldest spirits of the wood. They are often the first to sense unnatural blights or intrusions in the forest. Primarily made up of Wood Elves, they can be found deep within the densest parts of the Whispering Woods, bordering Alfiya territory, but always maintaining a respectful distance.

They are led by Walker Daehar Whispering-Pines, an ancient even for a Wood Elf, with bark-like skin and eyes that hold the stillness of a deep forest. His long, silver-streaked hair is interwoven with living moss. He moves with preternatural silence. He is patient, profoundly wise, and deeply attuned to the forest. He speaks in a soft, rustling voice, often in metaphors drawn from nature. He is highly cautious and values stealth and observation above all else. He is fiercely protective of the oldest parts of the Whispering Woods.

The Riverbend Wanderers

A convivial and adaptable clan, skilled in fishing, trapping, and crafting small, sturdy boats. They are excellent traders, often acting as intermediaries between other Wilder clans and cautiously approaching Towns like Olden's Ford for commerce. They are known for their lively storytelling and their ability to find safe passage along waterways. Primarily made up of Humans & Halflings, they follow the winding paths of major rivers and their tributaries, particularly those flowing through fertile plains.

Their walker is Kolra Riverbend, a vibrant woman in her late 40s, with a warm, open face and practical, sun-kissed skin. Her brown hair is often tied back with a simple leather thong. She wears practical, comfortable clothing suited for river travel. She is adaptable, resourceful, and highly sociable. She values community, trade, and the free flow of information. She is a skilled negotiator and storyteller, able to find common ground with almost anyone. She is pragmatic and always seeks the path of least resistance.

The Ironhide Pathfinders

A hardy and resilient clan, known for their strength, endurance, and pragmatic approach to survival. They are formidable hunters of large game and often serve as the vanguard for the larger Wilder movements. Their Walkers are typically powerful warriors who lead by example. They have a gruff but honest demeanour and are fiercely loyal to their kin. Their primary race are Half-Orcs, and they are found in more rugged, open plains and foothills, often skirting the edges of the Ash Wastes or minor mountain ranges.

Walker Crask Ironhide leads them, a powerfully built Half-Orc in his early 40s, with a rugged, scarred face and a quiet, watchful intensity in his eyes. He wears sturdy, utilitarian leather armour and carries a well-worn, heavy axe. He is gruff, direct, and utterly dependable. He leads by example, valuing strength, endurance, and unwavering loyalty. He has little patience for deception or weakness. He is fiercely protective of his clan and their hunting grounds.

The Sunstone Nomads

A cheerful and resourceful clan, master foragers and gatherers, with a knack for finding hidden springs and fertile patches. They are known for their comfortable, easily packed camps and their love of good food and communal feasts. They often act as the eyes of the Wilders, spotting distant movements from their vantage points. They are mainly halflings, and stick to lush grasslands and sunny, open plains, preferring areas with abundant forage and gentle hills.

Walker Tobar Sunstone, a nimble Halfling in his late 30s, with a perpetually cheerful, yet observant, expression. His clothes are often adorned with small pouches of foraged seeds and dried herbs. He moves with a light, almost skipping gait. Optimistic, resourceful, and deeply connected to the bounty of the land. He is a master forager and a natural diplomat, able to diffuse tension with a well-timed joke or a shared meal. He believes in finding joy in the simple things.

The Shadow-Stalkers

A reclusive and highly skilled clan, specializing in stealth, camouflage, and silent hunting. They are adept at setting traps and ambushes, and their scouts are unmatched in their ability to move unseen. They are often tasked with observing dangerous areas or tracking elusive threats. Found in dense, shadowy forests and areas with thick undergrowth, particularly those bordering less-travelled regions. The clan is made of humans and wood elves.

Reclusive, highly disciplined, and intensely focused. Walker Vonrol Shadow-Stalker values stealth, patience, and the art of observation above all else. He is a master of camouflage and silent movement, preferring to watch and gather information before acting. A lean, almost gaunt human in his late 50s, with eyes that are perpetually narrowed, as if constantly assessing shadows. He moves with an unnerving silence, his presence barely registering. He wears dark, patched clothing that blends seamlessly into the forest.

The Hearthfire Keepers

A traditional and communal clan, valuing storytelling, craftsmanship (particularly leatherwork and basic tool-making), and the warmth of the shared hearth. Their Walkers are often respected elders who prioritize the well-being and cohesion of the family unit. They are Humans known for their hospitality to other Wilders. They often find themselves in sheltered valleys and clearings, often near natural caves or rock formations that offer temporary shelter.

Walker Gareth Hearthfire is a sturdy, kind-faced man in his early 60s, with a warm smile and calloused hands. He has a comforting presence and often carries a small, intricately carved wooden pipe. He is traditional, communal, and deeply committed to the well-being of his clan. He values shared stories, warmth, and the simple comforts of life. He is a skilled artisan and storyteller, ensuring the clan's history is passed down. He is a voice of stability and common sense.

The Swiftfoot Scouts

A highly mobile and agile clan, specializing in rapid movement, long-distance scouting, and swift, precise hunting. They are often the messengers between other Wilder clans and are adept at avoiding confrontation through sheer speed and clever evasion. They are known for their light-heartedness and quick wit. They are Halflings & Wood Elves who prefer rolling hills and open, less dense woodlands, where their speed and agility can be fully utilized.

Wellie Swiftfoot is an energetic, quick-witted, and incredibly agile Halfling. She is in her early 30s, with bright, curious eyes and a perpetually ready stance. She wears light, practical clothing and is rarely seen without her running shoes. She thrives on movement and the freedom of the open lands. She is a natural messenger and scout, valuing speed and efficiency. She is often the first to bring news (good or bad) to the other clans.

The Stone-Echo Clan

A pragmatic and resourceful clan, with a surprising knack for rudimentary mining and identifying valuable minerals. They are skilled at setting up defensible camps within natural rock formations and are known for their sturdy, practical tools. Their Half-Orcs provide strength, while Gnomes offer ingenuity in navigating the rocky terrain. They prefer rocky foothills and areas with extensive cave systems or natural rock shelters, often near the fringes of smaller mountain ranges.

They are led by a meticulous Gnome in his late 110s, Brenn Stone-Echo. He has neatly combed hair and spectacles perched on his nose. He often has dust on his clothes from working with stone. He carries a small, polished stone that seems to hum faintly. He is pragmatic, resourceful, and deeply curious about the earth's secrets. He values ingenuity, sturdy craftsmanship, and the hidden patterns within stone. He is skilled at setting up defensible camps within natural rock formations and is known for their sturdy, practical tools.

The Sky-Gazer Clan

A band of Humans and Half-elves who prefer the high plain and open plateaus where they have an unobstructed view of the sky. A contemplative and observant clan, fascinated by the celestial phenomena of Geoctum. Their shamans and elders are often amateur astronomers, charting the flickering stars and interpreting the subtle shifts in the sky. They are known for their unique sky-lore and their ability to predict weather patterns, though the Everstorm remains a mystery.

Led by Dehlan Sky-Gazer, a walker who is intuitive, empathetic, and often contemplative. She is fascinated by the sky, sun and moons, interpreting the flickering stars and subtle atmospheric shifts as profound omens. She seeks spiritual understanding from the cosmos. She is a contemplative Half-Elf in her mid-50s, with luminous, silver eyes that often seem to gaze at distant horizons. She wears flowing, layered robes adorned with subtle astronomical symbols.

The Blood-Marked Hunters

A fierce and disciplined hunting clan, specializing in tracking and confronting dangerous beasts and hostile humanoids. They are known for their courage and their willingness to face down threats that others avoid. Their rituals often involve symbolic blood-marking after a successful hunt or battle. They are often the first line of defence for other Wilder clans against the Ironhide Warbands or Grimfang Nomads. They are Half-orcs and Humans that live in the more dangerous, untamed wilderness areas, often bordering regions frequented by monstrous humanoids.

A powerfully built Half-Orc leads this clan. Duroz Blood-Marked is in his early 30s, with a rugged face and a quiet, watchful intensity in his eyes. His skin is often streaked with natural pigments. He carries a massive, well-used hunting spear. He is quietly formidable, fiercely loyal, and deeply protective of his clan. He is pragmatic and direct, preferring action to lengthy debate. He despises anything that threatens his people or their way of life. He is a master hunter and a formidable combatant.

Relationship with other Nations

Mehdian Republic

The Wilders generally keep a respectful distance from Mehdian cities. They were formed during the Dark Age and have a deep distrust of the Mehdian. They view their expansion with suspicion but recognize their settlements as sources of crafted goods, certain foods, or information. Trade is often conducted at specific, agreed-upon trading trees or remote market clearings, rather than within Mehdian walls. They might occasionally hire themselves out as guides or trackers if the cause is just and the payment fair.

Alfiya

The Wood Elves within the Wilders feel a distant kinship with Alfiya, but prefer their freedom to Cosminas's rigid order. Alfiya's paternalistic view is generally unnoticed or ignored by the Wilders, who simply avoid the great city's borders. There's a mutual, if limited, respect for nature, though the Wilders might view Cosminas's living architecture as a powerful, but ultimately domesticated, form of nature.

The Stoneheart Empire

The Wilders avoid the mountains where possible, finding the Dwarven and Gnomish preference for stone and industry alien. Occasional skirmishes might occur if Wilder hunting parties stray too close to surface mines or Stonefist Sentinel patrols. They regard The Stoneheart Empire's warnings of the Underdark as a problem confined to the stone, not the living world.

Vanalir

They avoid Vanalir territory but might treat them with a guarded neutrality if encountered in neutral wilds, valuing their independence. They understand that the Vanalir are not demons, or “hellspawn” due to their encounters with them.

Ragged Crowns & Grimfang Nomads

These goblinoid factions are the most consistent threat and rival for the Wilders. Raids over hunting grounds, resources, and even sheer animosity are common. The Wilders employ guerrilla tactics, hit-and-run ambushes, and their knowledge of the terrain to outwit and evade the more numerous but less adaptable goblinoids.

Religion

For the Wilders, their view of the divine is shaped by two factors: their nomadic, survivalist lifestyle and the guidance of their Seer, who holds the "Communal Memory" of their ancestors, refugees who fled the Mad King's tyranny centuries ago. Their Gods do not speak to them, and the strong belief is because they were disconnected during the Dark Age.

While the Alfiyans "sing" to the gods and the Humans "plead" with a Saint, the Wilders treat the gods as if they are powerful, fickle neighbours. Their views are more accurate in the sense that they understand the gods as distinct, sometimes dangerous entities, rather than a single benevolent force like the Radiant Mother.

The Great Walker: Corellon Larethian

The Wood Elves within the Wilder clans don't see Corellon as a distant king in a crystal palace, but as the First Nomad. They believe Corellon didn't just lose his connection to the world, they believe he chose to go to ground, to hide from the Mad King’s Inquisition.

Every time a Wilder clan moves their camp, they leave a small token (a carved stone or a bit of dried meat) for the "Walker in the Shadows," hoping he is traveling alongside them.

The Lord of the Hunt: Malar

While the Alfiya revere the beauty of the forest, the Wilders revere its lethality. They see the "Old God of the Hunt" as the one who provides game but demands respect in return. The Alfiya find this worship "savage," but the Wilders believe the Elves' bio-magic is an insult to the raw, untamed nature of the Old Gods.

The Sky-Seer: Selûne

The Wilders rely on the stars and the moon for navigation and timing their migrations. They view Selûne not as a goddess of "Knowledge" in a library, but as the Original Seer. They believe the Veil Tears are her "Winking Eyes" trying to guide them through the darkness. Because they spend every night under the open sky, they are often the first to notice new Tears, giving them a more accurate (if superstitious) understanding of the Veil’s breakdown than the city-bound humans.

The Night-Steward: Shar

While the civilized world fears the dark, the Wilders embrace it as their primary camouflage. To them, Shar is not a villain, but the Matron of the Long Night. The Wilders believe Shar is the one who "erased the tracks" of their ancestors when they fled the Mad King. They see her as Pragmatic Silence. To a Wilder, loss isn't a tragedy, it’s a shedding of weight. To survive the nomad life, you must be able to lose things, possessions, memories, even kin, without being slowed down.

When a Wilder dies or a great tragedy strikes the clan, they do not build monuments. Instead, they burn what remains and scatter the ashes in a place they vow never to return to. They ask Shar to "swallow the memory" so the grief doesn't become a burden that prevents the clan from moving fast enough to survive.

The Earth-Mother: Chauntea

The Wilders reject the Mehdian view of Chauntea as a goddess of farming and ripening grain. To them, she is the Giver of Roots. They don't plant crops, they forage with permission. They believe that if they stay in one place too long (like the settled races), they are strangling the Mother, which is why they must remain nomadic.

The Storm-Bringer: Talos

While the people of Nestport fear the Everstorm as a divine punishment, the Wilders see Talos as the Great Cleaner. When a massive storm hits, the Wilders don't hide in stone cellars, they move to high ground and offer War-Songs to the sky. They believe the storm is the gods trying to blow away the Veil, and they welcome its arrival even as it threatens their lives.
