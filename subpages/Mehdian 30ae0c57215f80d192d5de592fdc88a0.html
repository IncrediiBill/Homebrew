TextThe Mehdian Republic is a prominent nation on Geoctum, transitioning from a tyrannical human-only power to a beacon of reconciliation and stability. Following the death of the Mad King, a new capital named Valoria was established, promoting internal development and trade while facing internal threats from loyalists seeking to restore the old regime. The Council of Regents, comprising leaders focused on military, cultural affairs, and the common people's welfare, aims to rebuild trust and foster peace, despite ongoing challenges with neighboring nations and internal factions. The Mehdian faith, centered around the Radiant Mother, emphasizes community and hope, influencing daily life and governance.

The Mehdian (Meh-dian) Republic is the dominant nation on Geoctum. Once the continent-spanning, tyrannical human only power of the Dark Age, it now endeavours to be a beacon of reconciliation and stability. This duality defines its current existence, as it attempts to forge a new identity while grappling with the long shadow of the Mad King's rule. A more distributed presence, likely centred around their original capital in a fertile plain, with other cities acting as regional hubs.

Current Political Climate & Relations

The Mehdian's current political landscape is a complex tapestry of reform, lingering resentment, and a determined effort to rebuild trust.

The death of the King shattered the Mehdian command structure and brought an end to the war. In the aftermath, the victorious Alliance made a profound symbolic choice. Viewing Eastway as a place tainted by tyranny, they chose to abandon it as the seat of power.

To signify a true new beginning, they established a new capital, Valoria, in the fertile Mehdian heartland, and opened their citizenship to races from all over the continent. This new city and opening to other races, built with open architecture and dedicated to peace, became the home of the newly formed Council of Regents. They focus on internal development, trade, and diplomacy. However, their authority doesn't extend far beyond their city borders, leading to a focus on making their core cities exemplary.

The Mehdian's most significant internal threat in the Echoes of the Empire. The current government is constantly vigilant against these clandestine loyalists who seek to restore the Mad King's oppressive rule. This struggle often manifests as political manoeuvring, accusations of treason, and quiet disappearances within the capital. The Mehdian fears a resurgence of the Dark Age more than any external threat.

Council of Regents

Council Leader: High Regent Myrna Netsk

A woman in her late 50s, with a dignified bearing, wise, compassionate eyes, and streaks of grey in her neatly braided brown hair. She often wears robes of deep blue or green, embroidered with symbols of peace and growth. She is the most prominent and influential voice on the Council, often acting as its public face. Myrna embodies the hopes of the Free Age.

She is genuinely committed to the ideals of peace, reconciliation, and rebuilding Mehdian's reputation. She believes profoundly in the inherent goodness of humanity and strives to foster positive relations with other races. She is a skilled orator and diplomat, though perhaps a little naive about the true depth of lingering resentments, both from other races and within her own. She trusts in due process and legal reform.

Myrna is, however, vigilant against the Echoes of the Old Empire, seeing them as a dangerous and misguided threat to Mehdian's very soul.

Regent Commander Theron Wolfclaw

The Council's voice for military, defense, and resource management. He oversees the Mehdian military and the protection of trade routes.

A burly, grizzled man in his early 60s, a veteran commander with a neatly trimmed beard and a stern, weathered face. He often wears practical, well-maintained uniform attire, even in Council meetings, always with a sheathed sword at his hip.

A pragmatist above all else. He believes in strength as a deterrent and is wary of showing weakness. While he supports the Council's goals of peace, he views it as a fragile state that must be constantly guarded. He is deeply loyal to the Mehdian people and will prioritize their safety above all else, even if it means unpopular decisions. He's tired of war but recognizes its necessity.

He comes from a line of great warriors, and his ancestor served during the tail end of the Mad King's reign but was quickly instrumental in the coup that deposed him, seeing the King's madness as a threat to Mehdian's very existence. He believes it's imperative to learn from it, not simply forget it.

He's concerned by the rising banditry from Ragged Crowns and Grimfang Nomads impacting trade. He's also acutely aware of the Echoes of the Old Empire, seeing them as an internal cancer that must be excised for Mehdian's survival. He would be the one pushing for active investigation and counterintelligence against them.

Regent Master Eldrin Blackwood

Eldrin is the Regent responsible for cultural affairs and historical preservation. He frequently speaks of the importance of learning from the past and maintaining tradition. A man in his early 50s, with a lean build, sharp features, and a precise, almost scholarly demeanour. He has a precise, resonant voice and favours rich, traditional Mehdian attire that hints at a noble lineage.

He is calm, collected, and highly articulate, often sounding reasonable and wise. He stresses the importance of order and stability above all else. He frequently laments the chaos of the Free Age and the loss of respect for authority.

Regent Speaker Bella Cadence

Bella is the voice of the common people and urban trades. She champions policies that benefit merchants, artisans, and the working class, often pushing for pragmatic solutions to daily problems like food prices or housing. A lively woman in her late 30s, with a quick smile and expressive hands. She dresses in well-made but practical clothing, eschewing noble finery. Her dark auburn hair is often tied back loosely.

Her family suffered economic hardship during the Mad King's later years, forcing them to work their way up. She views the Dark Age as a time of unnecessary suffering that must never be repeated, and distrusts anything that smacks of unchecked aristocratic power.

She is the most direct and approachable council member, and is fiercely protective of the populace. She's less concerned with grand historical narratives or military strategy, focusing instead on the tangible well-being of the Mehdian citizens. She can be stubborn and distrustful of high-minded ideals that don't translate to real-world improvements. She's a strong advocate for public works and stable trade.

Regent Lumina Veridia

Lumina is the Council's expert on magical affairs, obscure lore, and diplomatic relations with non-human factions, particularly Alfiya. She often serves as the bridge between pragmatic governance and arcane considerations. A serene woman in her early 30s, with intelligent, often distant, silver eyes and long, straight black hair. She wears simple, unadorned robes, sometimes embroidered with subtle astronomical or magical symbols. She has a quiet, thoughtful demeanour.

She comes from a long line of scholars who suffered during the Mad King's purges of independent thought. She believes the loss of historical records is the greatest crime of that era and is dedicated to re-establishing academic freedom and expanding knowledge.

She is highly intelligent, inquisitive, and methodical. She approaches problems from a scholarly perspective, valuing knowledge and understanding. She is often more open-minded to non-human perspectives than her peers, believing there's much to learn from other cultures. She has a keen, intuitive grasp of magical flows and ancient mysteries.

Relationship with other Nations

Alfiya

The Mehdian leadership offers constant overtures of peace and reparations to Cosminas, acknowledging the atrocities of the Dark Age. However, these efforts are often met with glacial slowness and profound distrust from the Elves, who remember every slight. Mehdian diplomats are often frustrated by what they perceive as Elven "unwillingness to move on," unaware of the depth of the Elven memory or their true reasons for caution.

Younger generations of Mehdian genuinely feel no ill will and are often at the forefront of efforts to build bridges, often by establishing trade routes to mixed-race towns near Elven territories or participating in joint cultural festivals.

Stoneheart Empire

Trade exists, with Mehdian agriculture and crafts exchanging for Dwarven metals and engineering. However, the Dwarves' insularity and their memory of Dark Age raids create a constant undercurrent of suspicion. The Mehdian would be unaware of the growing Drow threat The Stoneheart Empire faces, and likely dismiss Dwarven warnings of "things that tunnel too deep" as hyperbole.

The Wilders

The Mehdian generally views the Wilders as unpredictable and somewhat uncivilized. They rarely interfere unless Wilder actions directly threaten Mehdian trade routes or settlements. Some Mehdian might try to civilise or settle Wilder clans, which is often met with resistance.

The Vanalir

The Mehdian know the Vanalir as "The Hellspawn" and largely avoid their southern territories. Superstition runs high, with common folk spreading exaggerated tales of demonic rituals. Official Mehdian policy is non-interference, viewing them as a problem best left contained.

Dragonborn Clans

The Mehdian Empire clashed often with the Dragonborn clans when discovered, which has resulted in negative interactions even with the reformed Republic. Tensions are always high when they find the Dragonborn clans and they are wary to avoid their territory, especially in the north where they can be found close to the city of Icedrift.

Religion

The Mehdian faith, known as the Theocracy of the Radiant Mother, is a centralized religion that arose from the ashes of a forgotten history. It is a faith of practical piety, focused on community, hope, and the bounty of the earth, which is seen as a direct gift from their divine patron.

The core tenet of the Mehdian faith is the belief in a singular, divine saint known as the Radiant Mother. In a world where the old gods are distant and silent, her direct intervention in the distant past is the foundation of their faith. Mehdians believe the Radiant Mother appeared to their ancestors in a time of great peril, not with fire and fury, but with the quiet promise of a new day. She is venerated as the source of all life-giving things—the fertile soil, the ripening grain, and the enduring strength of the Mehdian people.

The religion teaches that the virtues of compassion, vigilance, and honesty are not just moral guides but divine commands, given to the people to ensure their survival and prosperity. The Theocracy preaches a doctrine of shared purpose, where every individual's labour, from the farmer's toil to the priest's prayer, contributes to the collective well-being.

The Radiant Mother

According to their sacred texts, the Radiant Mother was a divine saint who, in the dark and dangerous days of the early world, descended from a realm of pure light. She found the ancestors of the Mehdian’s lost and broken, but gave them a "teardrop of radiant light" that gave them the will to build and cultivate. She taught them how to draw life from the land and forge a new society.
She has not made a direct appearance in an age, but the hope of her return is a powerful undercurrent in Mehdian society.

The Cathedral of Radiant Tears

The Cathedral of Radiant Tears in Itean is the physical and spiritual heart of this faith. The teardrop-shaped dome of stained glass represents the "teardrop of radiant light" the Radiant Mother gifted to their ancestors. It symbolises both the grief of a lost past and the immense hope she brought to them. The light that floods the cathedral's interior is a physical manifestation of her enduring presence, a constant reminder of the gift she bestowed upon them.

The Theocracy

The faith is governed by a strict but benevolent Theocracy, headquartered in Itean. At its head is the Grand Lume, the spiritual leader who serves as the voice and interpreter of the Radiant Mother’s teachings.

The Grand Lume presides over a council of High Priests, each of whom embodies one of the core virtues of the faith:

  • Kaia, High Priestess of Compassion: Oversees charitable works and inter-racial diplomacy.
  • Cassian, High Priest of Vigilance: Guards against external and internal threats to the city and faith.
  • Soriana, High Priestess of Honesty: Arbitrates disputes and ensures ethical conduct within the guilds and markets.

This structure ensures that the faith is not just a set of beliefs, but a living, breathing part of Mehdian daily life, influencing everything from politics to trade

Practices and Rituals

Mehdian piety is a practical matter. Prayers are often short, heartfelt thanks for a good harvest or a safe journey. Major religious ceremonies are tied to the seasons and the agricultural cycle. The most important is the Festival of First Light, a grand celebration that takes place at the spring equinox. During this festival, farmers bring the first seeds of the year to the Cathedral to be blessed by the Grand Lume. Other daily rituals include a communal meal at sunset, where families say a prayer of thanks for the day’s blessings and offer a small portion of their food to the Radiant Mother.

The Radiant Blades

The Radiant Blades serves as the zealous military arm of the Theocracy, a brotherhood and sisterhood of paladins and clerics utterly devoted to the Radiant Mother and the principles of the Mehdian faith. They are the strongest and most devout individuals, chosen for their unwavering faith, martial prowess, and commitment to justice as defined by the Theocracy.

They handle internal threats to the faith, such as rooting out heresy or dealing with cults, and provide a heavily armoured presence for important religious ceremonies or the transport of sacred relics. While the city watch handles common crime, the Radiant Blades deals with issues that directly challenge the spiritual authority or physical safety of the Theocracy itself. They are also tasked with protecting the Grand Lume and the High Priests.

Members of the Radiant Blades wear heavy, polished plate armour, often with intricate golden trim that reflects light, giving them a truly radiant appearance. Their helmets are typically simple, unadorned visors, allowing their faces to be seen, emphasizing their humanity and devotion. They carry well-maintained swords, maces, or polearms, often etched with symbols of the Radiant Mother, such as the teardrop or a stylized sheaf of wheat. Their cloaks are typically a deep, rich blue, symbolizing the sky where the Radiant Mother is believed to reside.

When on guard within the Cathedral, they don a special golden ceremonial armour. This includes a full plate helmet designed with an androgynous human face and stylized wings on the back of their breastplate, symbolizing purity and divine ascent. Instead of cloaks, they wear leather battle skirts of the same deep, rich blue over their armour which flow from their waist to below the knee, with a gap at the front to show off their golden armour.

Their primary and ultimate loyalty is to the Radiant Mother herself. While they serve the Grand Lume and the Theocracy in her absence, their oaths are sworn directly to the divine saint. The belief that the Radiant Mother could one day return is a core tenet of their order, and they are prepared to be her sworn defenders, abandoning all other allegiances should she manifest. This singular devotion makes them both incredibly powerful and, potentially, a force that could act independently if they believed the Theocracy had strayed from the Radiant Mother's true will.
