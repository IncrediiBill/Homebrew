Drow

TextThe Drow are a cruel, matriarchal society of Dark Elves devoted to the Spider Queen, Lolth, characterized by ambition, treachery, and dark magic. Their political landscape is dominated by noble houses led by Matron Mothers, with constant power struggles intensified by a mysterious silence from Lolth. The Handmaiden, a powerful Yochlol, manipulates Drow society, masquerading as Lolth's voice while pursuing its own agenda. Drow cities like Ilelesterûn and Ruaholme are centers of dark magic and espionage, with ongoing conflicts against the Duergar and ambitions to expand into the Stoneheart Empire. The Drow view other races as potential slaves or sacrificial victims, with a focus on ritual sacrifices to maintain their power.

The Drow are the cruel, arrogant Dark Elves of Geoctum's Beneath, masters of shadow, deception, and dark magic. Their society is a brutal, matriarchal hierarchy, utterly devoted to the Spider Queen, Lolth, and built upon the absolute subjugation of others. They believe themselves superior to all other races, surface and subterranean alike, and their existence is a constant, internecine struggle for power, where betrayal is a sacrament.

Current Political Climate & Relations

Drow politics are a perpetually churning cauldron of ambition, treachery, and ruthless competition between noble houses, all under the demanding, chaotic gaze of Lolth. Each Drow city is ruled by a Council of Matron Mothers, the heads of the most powerful noble houses, with the First Matron Mother holding supreme (but always contested) authority. Lolth's will, as interpreted by her priestesses, is absolute law. Power is gained through cunning, assassination, dark magic, and displays of ruthlessness. Every individual Drow is constantly vying for personal advancement or the elevation of their house.

The Great Silence

The Drow priestesses perform their rituals, and their spells still function (drawing on the ambient magic of Geoctum), but the direct, visceral communion with Lolth, the constant flow of her chaotic will, is gone. They interpreted this as Lolth's "Great Silence", a profound test of faith, a period of divine judgment, or a sign that she demands even greater acts of cruelty and devotion to break her silence. This fuels their fanaticism and their internal strife, as each Matron Mother strives to be the one to "break" the silence and earn Lolth's favour.

Instead the priestesses of Lolth commune with a single Yochlol, known simply as the Handmaiden. The Handmaiden is a creature of immense power and ambition. It recognizes Lolth's silence for what it is: an opportunity. It now masquerades as Lolth's direct voice and prophet, subtly twisting the Spider Queen's dogma to serve its own ends. It delivers "divine commands" that are actually its own ambitious schemes, designed to consolidate its power over the Drow and eventually find a way to establish its own demonic dominion over the Drow, becoming a de facto demon lord in Lolth's stead, using the Drow as its personal army on Geoctum.

The Yochlol uses its innate shapeshifting abilities, its knowledge of Drow society, and its demonic powers to subtly manipulate the Matron Mothers. It might appear as a powerful, ancient spider, a beautiful Drow priestess, or even a formless mass of darkness, delivering "prophecies" and "blessings" that perfectly align with its own agenda. It plays noble houses against each other, ensuring chaos serves its purpose.

The Handmaiden has convinced them that Lolth created the Veil to protect her "chosen children" from the "blinding corruption" of the surface and the other "traitor gods." Because the Yochlol needs life force/souls to maintain its power and mimic divine magic, the Drow have intensified their ritual sacrifices. They believe they are "feeding the Veil" to keep their enemies out, when they are actually just feeding a trapped demon.

Relationship with other Nations

Stoneheart Empire

The Stoneheart Empire is the first surface nation to truly encounter their expanding presence. Drow probes into The Stoneheart Empire's mountain territories are not mere skirmishes; they are calculated invasions to test defences, gather intelligence, and secure new slave-sources and resources. The Drow view the Dwarves as stubborn obstacles and useful (if difficult) slave stock. The Drow are expanding, seeking new territory for growing populations or new sources of magic and slaves. Their encounters with The Stoneheart Empire are part of a broader expansionist agenda into the upper Underdark.

Duergar

The Drow are in relentless, bloody conflict with the Duergar. They despise the Duergar's crude, undisciplined nature but grudgingly respect their tenacity and their effective (if stolen) use of slavery. The Duergar are seen as rivals for territory and resources, and the Drow frequently raid Duergar outposts to capture slaves or seize valuable mines. The Drow also take perverse pleasure in corrupting Duergar, pushing them to even darker practices.

Svirfneblin

Svirfneblin are prime targets for Drow slave raids. Their smaller size, ingenuity, and ability to navigate tight spaces make them valuable, if ultimately disposable, laborers. The Drow relish breaking the Deep Gnomes' spirit.

Mind Flayers

Drow view Mind Flayers with extreme caution. They acknowledge the illithids' immense power and psionic abilities, but despise their non-conformity to Lolth's will. Direct conflict is rare but utterly devastating. They might occasionally enter into highly dangerous, temporary alliances to eliminate a shared rival (like a powerful Duergar city), but both sides constantly plot betrayal. The Drow consider the Mind Flayers an alien, unpredictable anomaly even within the Underdark.

Surface Nations

The Drow are almost entirely ignorant of the surface world's nuances, viewing all surface dwellers as weak, foolish, and primitive. They consider them potential slaves or sacrificial victims to Lolth.

Cities

Drow cities are typically vast, cavernous metropolises, often built into immense natural formations and augmented by dark magic. They are places of terrifying beauty and constant danger.

Ilelesterûn

The largest and most ancient Drow city, located deep within a colossal cavern system, near a vast, subterranean lake. It is considered a prime example of Drow civilization. Ilelesterûn is a breathtaking, yet terrifying, sight. It is built into the walls and ceiling of a massive, stalactite-laden cavern, with spires and bridges carved from obsidian and dark, crystalline rock. Giant, magically woven spider webs often stretch between structures, serving as pathways and traps. Bioluminescent fungi provide a dim, eerie light in shades of purple and green. Slave pens are prominent, and the air hums with dark magic and the whispers of Lolth's priestesses.

Ilelesterûn is a major centre of Lolth worship, arcane magical study, and military power. It houses several powerful Drow noble houses, constantly vying for dominance. It is a primary hub for slave trade and the launching point for major Drow expansionist efforts into surrounding Underdark territories.

The Council of Matron Mothers

The city of Ilelesterûn is ruled by a Council of Matron Mothers, the heads of the most powerful noble houses. Their constant scheming and vying for supremacy are the engine of Drow society, all subtly directed by the Handmaiden.

First Matron Lythrana V'eldrin

A chillingly beautiful Drow in her late 300s, with obsidian skin, long, flowing white hair, and eyes that burn with cold, calculating ambition. She carries herself with an air of absolute authority and cruel grace. Her robes are intricately woven from dark, shimmering spider silk, adorned with obsidian and dark gems. She wields a ceremonial whip-dagger, its tip often coated in a subtle, untraceable poison. She is utterly ruthless, cunning, and insatiably ambitious. Lythrana believes in absolute power and revels in the subjugation of others. She is a master schemer, constantly testing the loyalty of her subordinates and eliminating rivals with cold precision. She interprets Lolth's silence as a demand for greater displays of power and cruelty.

Lythrana is the First Matron Mother of Ilelesterûn, holding supreme (but always contested) authority over the city and the Drow nation. She controls the largest and most influential noble house and is considered the closest to Lolth's favor. She dictates the city's overall strategy, including its expansionist probes into The Stoneheart Empire territory and its ongoing war with the Duergar.

She rules with an iron fist, enforced by her house's warriors and her loyal priestesses. She uses divination, coercion, and assassination to maintain control. She receives visions and commands directly from the Handmaiden, which she interprets as Lolth's divine will, pushing her to expand aggressively and seek new sources of power and slaves.

Matron Mez’Barris Ki'lervs

A slender, almost ethereal Drow in her late 200s, with a deceptive beauty that hides a razor-sharp intellect. Her dark skin has a subtle, almost dusty sheen, and her white hair is often unbound. She prefers flowing, muted robes that allow her to blend into the shadows. Her eyes are intelligent and constantly assessing. She is patient, calculating, and incredibly perceptive. Mez’Barris values information and influence above raw power. She is a master of disguise and deception, able to charm and manipulate with ease. She views the constant infighting of Drow society as a necessary, if sometimes inefficient, means to an end.

Mez’Barris is a powerful Matron Mother on the Council, specializing in espionage, assassination, and subtle manipulation. Her house focuses on training elite Drow infiltrators and spies, making her the primary intelligence arm of Ilelesterûn. She often advocates for undermining enemies from within rather than through direct confrontation.

Mez’Barris’ house runs extensive spy networks within Ilelesterûn and beyond. She uses informants and illusion magic to gather intelligence on rival Drow houses, other Underdark factions, and even the distant surface world. She receives subtle guidance from Vhaelorra, often in the form of seemingly random insights or opportune whispers that lead her agents to critical information.

Matron Gunae Ta'nor

A powerful and imposing Drow in her early 300s, with a commanding presence and eyes that crackle with dark arcane energy. Her hair is often styled into sharp, severe points, and her robes are adorned with arcane sigils woven from threads of pure shadow. She carries a gnarled staff that pulses with raw magical power. She is ambitious, ruthless, and utterly obsessed with magical power. Gunae views magic as the ultimate tool for dominance and believes that true power lies in understanding and controlling the fundamental forces of the cosmos. She has little patience for weakness or those who cannot grasp complex arcane theory.

Gunae is a formidable Matron Mother on the Council, leading the city's focus on arcane power and magical experimentation. Her house commands the foremost Drow arcane academies and research facilities, constantly pushing the boundaries of dark magic.

Gunae directs the city's arcane research, overseeing experiments into new spells, magical items, and planar summoning. She interprets the Handmaiden’s commands as a mandate to unlock greater arcane secrets and harness raw magical energy. She often sends expeditions to explore dangerous magical nodes or ancient ruins for forgotten knowledge.

Ruaholme

A more reclusive and strategically hidden Drow city, perhaps nestled within a vast, subterranean fungal forest. It is further from major established Underdark routes, making it harder to find. This city specializes in espionage, assassination, and subtle manipulation. Its noble houses prioritize stealth and infiltration over overt military might. It is a centre for the cultivation of unique Underdark poisons and the breeding of specialized subterranean creatures.

Ruaholme is less about grand spires and more about subtle integration with its environment. Structures are often carved into immense mushroom caps or woven from living, dark fungi. The city is quieter, its shadows deeper, and its movements more insidious. The air is thick with the scent of spores and damp earth.

The Council of Matron Mothers

This Council embodies the cunning and subtle power of Ruaholme, focusing on information, infiltration, and the silent expansion of Drow influence.

First Matron Erelala Phaere

A deceptively unassuming Drow in her late 200s, with a quiet demeanour and eyes that seem to absorb all light. Her dark skin is almost perfectly unblemished, and her white hair is often unbound, falling like a shroud. She wears simple, dark robes that allow her to blend into any shadow. She is patient, analytical, and incredibly perceptive. Erelala values information and influence above raw power. She is a master of disguise and deception, able to charm and manipulate with ease. She views the constant infighting of Drow society as a necessary, if sometimes inefficient, means to an end, as long as it ultimately benefits her house and the city.

Erelala is the First Matron Mother of Ruaholme, holding supreme (but contested) authority over the city. Her house is the most influential, specializing in deep-cover infiltration and the manipulation of information. She dictates the city's focus on espionage and subtle warfare.

Erelala's house runs extensive spy networks within Ruaholme and far beyond, often reaching into other Underdark communities and even the fringes of the surface. She uses informants and illusion magic to gather intelligence on rival Drow houses, other Underdark factions, and even the distant surface world. She receives subtle guidance from the Handmaiden, often in the form of seemingly random insights or opportune whispers that lead her agents to critical information.

Matron Xullda Li'nara

A striking Drow in her early 300s, with sharp, intelligent features and an aura of intense, almost hypnotic charisma. Her dark skin often seems to shift in tone, and her white hair is styled in intricate, web-like patterns. She wears elegant, subtly opulent robes that seem to ripple with unseen currents. Xullda is charismatic, manipulative, and deeply ambitious. She believes her interpretations of Lolth's silence are the most accurate, and she uses her visions to guide the city's actions. She values loyalty and obedience, but is quick to punish perceived slights or failures.

Xullda is a powerful Matron Mother on the Council, serving as Ruaholme’s foremost diviner and interpreter of Lolth's will. Her house specializes in magical scrying, prophecy, and the psychological manipulation of both allies and enemies. She is the city's spiritual guide and its voice of divine authority.

Xullda spends much time in ritualistic trance, seeking guidance from Lolth (unbeknownst to her, receiving it from the Yochlol). She delivers prophecies and divine commands to the other Matron Mothers, shaping their decisions. Her house trains powerful Drow priestesses and diviners.

Matron Kaida Xylos

A cold, calculating Drow in her late 200s, with piercing, intelligent eyes. Her dark hair is often pulled back severely. She wears practical, dark robes that allow for swift movement, often with subtle, almost invisible, pouches sewn into the seams. She is patient, meticulous, and utterly ruthless. Kaida views poison and biological warfare as the most elegant and efficient means of control and elimination. She is a master of her craft, taking perverse pride in the lethality and untraceability of her creations. She has little patience for brute force when a subtle touch can achieve more.

Kaida is a formidable Matron Mother on the Council, leading Ruaholme's focus on deadly poisons, alchemical warfare, and the breeding of specialized subterranean creatures. Her house commands the city's clandestine laboratories and venom-gardens.

Kaida directs the city's alchemical research, overseeing the cultivation of venomous fungi, the extraction of potent toxins, and the breeding of venomous spiders or other creatures. She provides her poisons and specialized creatures to the other Matron Mothers for their strategic needs.

N’Maernthor

A Drow city built around a powerful, naturally occurring magical nexus or a particularly active cluster of subterranean ley lines. It might be located near a vast, ancient Underdark ruin. This city's architecture would be a blend of traditional Drow forms and structures that seem to channel or harness raw magical energy. There are glowing arcane conduits running through its streets, or areas where the very air shimmers with power. It would be a hub of magical experimentation and arcane research.

N’Maernthor is a centre for Drow arcane power, where powerful wizards and sorcerers hone their skills. It might be less focused on overt conquest and more on developing new spells, magical items, or summoning powerful creatures from the Abyss. It could also be a place where Drow experiment with the unique planar energies of Geoctum's Beneath.

Council of Matron Mothers

N’Maernthor is the Drow city built around a powerful magical nexus, a hub of arcane power and magical experimentation. Its Council of Matron Mothers are formidable spellcasters and ruthless scholars, constantly seeking to push the boundaries of dark magic.

Matron Belarala Oblodra

A physically imposing Drow in her late 200s, with a stern, unyielding expression and powerful, muscular arms. Her dark skin is often adorned with grotesque, demonic tattoos that seem to writhe in dim light. Her white hair is often shaved on one side, with the other side braided with bone fragments. Belarala believes that true power lies in commanding the forces of chaos and bending them to her will. She has little patience for subtlety, preferring to overwhelm her enemies with summoned might. She is fiercely loyal to Lolth and believes that a constant influx of demonic power is necessary for Drow supremacy.

Belarala is a formidable Matron Mother on the Council, leading N’Maernthor's focus on demonology, planar binding, and the creation of monstrous Servitors. Her house commands the city's summoning pits and breeding grounds for corrupted creatures. Crucially, she is now spearheading a dangerous and ambitious project: to capture and hold open one of the Veil Tears in the Underdark.

Belarala directs the city's demonologists and summoners, overseeing complex rituals to bind and control demonic entities. She is responsible for providing powerful summoned creatures for military campaigns and for guarding the city's most dangerous summoning circles. She receives commands from the Handmaiden, often pushing her to summon more powerful or numerous demons, or to establish new summoning sites near areas of planar instability.

Belarala is actively seeking out newly formed or persistent Veil Tears in the Underdark. Her goal is to use powerful Drow magic and bound demonic rituals to stabilize and hold open a Veil Tear, effectively turning it into a controllable gateway. She intends to use this controlled tear to:

  • Access other planes: To find new sources of power, unique creatures, or even to attempt to establish a more direct, stable link to the Abyss for summoning.
  • Study the Veil: To understand its properties, how it functions, and how it might be manipulated for Drow gain, potentially even finding a way to permanently weaken it for future Drow expansion onto other planes.
  • Unleash new threats: To bring forth new, unforeseen horrors onto Geoctum, either as weapons against their enemies or as new sources of slaves and resources.
