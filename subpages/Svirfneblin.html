TextSvirfneblin, the resourceful Deep Gnomes of Geoctum's Beneath, prioritize survival and community protection against threats like the Duergar and Drow. They maintain a cautious alliance with The Stoneheart Empire for mutual support. Their religion revolves around the Old Gods, particularly Callarduran Smoothhands, whom they believe protects them through the earth. Svirfneblin fear Mind Flayers and avoid conflict, resorting to sabotage against their oppressors. They possess hidden artifacts of their history but keep them secret to avoid discovery.

The Svirfneblin are the quiet, often dour, but incredibly resourceful Deep Gnomes of Geoctum's Beneath. Driven by their innate love of gems, intricate craftsmanship, and an aversion to open conflict, they strive for peaceful (or at least undisturbed) coexistence in the most dangerous environment imaginable. They are often victims of the Underdark's harsher denizens, particularly the Duergar who enslave them, making their existence a constant struggle for survival and quiet ingenuity.

Current Political Climate & Relations

Svirfneblin politics are highly localized and defensive, focused on survival, evasion, and the protection of their communities. Unity is often born of shared oppression. Svirfneblin cities (or more accurately, extensive, hidden enclaves) are often led by a revered Stone-Speaker, chosen for their wisdom, their skill in engineering or gem-crafting, and their ability to keep the community safe and hidden. Decisions are made with extreme caution and consensus, valuing subtlety and long-term survival over overt displays of power.

Surface Nations

Svirfneblin view surface dwellers with intense suspicion. They find surface environments blinding and overwhelming. Any surface creature venturing into their hidden territories would be met with extreme caution, stealthy observation, and likely attempts to simply lead them away.

The Stoneheart Empire

Free Svirfneblin communities maintain a fragile, often clandestine, alliance with The Stoneheart Empire. They often exchange vital information about Duergar and Drow movements for limited aid, such as basic supplies, tools, or (rarely) direct military intervention if a Svirfneblin community is under existential threat. The Stoneheart Empire views them as unfortunate kin and a useful source of intelligence, a stark contrast to their disdain for Duergar.

Many enslaved Svirfneblin dream of escaping to The Stoneheart Empire. Their escape attempts are dangerous, and the Dwarves sometimes quietly facilitate "underground railroads" for them.

Duergar

The Duergar are the Svirfneblin's most feared and hated enemies. The Duergar relentlessly raid Svirfneblin settlements for slaves, who are then brutally exploited in mines and workshops. Svirfneblin avoid direct conflict whenever possible, but acts of sabotage, daring rescues, and guerrilla warfare against their enslavers are common.

Drow

Svirfneblin fear the Drow deeply, recognizing their cruelty and power. They consider the Drow even more monstrous than the Duergar due to their chaotic nature and arcane prowess. Svirfneblin go to extraordinary lengths to hide their settlements from Drow patrols, using their natural camouflage and innate magic. They view any Drow expansion as a growing blight.

Mind Flayers

Mind Flayers are the ultimate horror to Svirfneblin. They understand the threat of psionic domination and brain-eating. Any Svirfneblin who discovers a Mind Flayer presence will do everything in their power to hide, flee, or, in extreme desperation, alert a stronger (and less threatening) force like the The Stoneheart Empire Dwarves. Their communities are often riddled with anti-psionic wards.

Religion

They remember the Old Gods not as grand figures, but as "The Quiet Protectors" who once helped them hide from the horrors of the deep.

The Primary Patron: Callarduran Smoothhands

They believe Callarduran didn't leave because of the Veil; they believe he became the stone to protect them. To a Deep Gnome, every vein of gemstone is a fingerprint of their god, and every dead-end tunnel is a "shield" he placed to lead their enemies astray. They have a distorted view of his "Hands”. They believe that if they make too much noise or build too grand a city (like the Alfiya or the Humans), Callarduran will stop hiding them, and the "Gaze of the Veil" will find them. This makes them extreme isolationists.

The Watcher in the Dark: Segojan Earthcaller (Nature/Life)

They see Segojan as the one who speaks to the roots of the world. Svirfneblin gardeners tend to massive fungal forests. They don't pray with words; they pray by tuning the vibrations of the earth. They believe that as the Veil breaks, the earth is shivering, and they are desperately trying to soothe Segojan to keep their tunnels from collapsing.

The Distorted Enemy: Urdlen (The Crawler Below)

They believe the Veil Tears are actually the "Claw Marks of Urdlen" trying to dig his way into Geoctum. This makes them terrified of the "Tears" that the Wilders or Vanalir might find fascinating. If a Svirfneblin sees a rift in the sky or a cave wall, they don't look through it, they seal the tunnel and run.

Knowledge and the "Forgotten Script”

Because the Mad King’s purges mostly affected the surface, the Svirfneblin actually possess some of the most accurate physical artifacts of the Old Gods (idols, tablets, and carvings).

However, they are so afraid of being discovered that they bury their own history. Their most accurate records of the Old Gods are hidden in "Ghost Deposits", treasure caches encased in magical stone that only a Svirfneblin's "Stone-Speech" can open.
