Ragged Crowns

TagsBugbearsGoblinsHobgoblinsOgres
TextThe goblinoids of Geoctum, known as the Ragged Crowns, are divided into tribes led by self-proclaimed Blood Kings and Queens, each with conflicting claims to royalty. Key tribes include the Iron Legion, known for their discipline and ambition to unite the goblinoids; the Skull-Throne Kingdom, which seeks legitimacy through conquest; and the Gutter-Scourge Tribe, characterized by chaotic raiding. Other tribes like the Blighted Fang and Rust-Eater Clan showcase unique leadership and resourcefulness. Each tribe has distinct settlements and goals, reflecting their varied philosophies and traditions.

The goblinoids of Geoctum, known collectively as the Ragged Crowns, are not a unified force. They are a collection of tribes and minor kingdoms, each led by a self-proclaimed Blood King or Queen, and each clinging to a distorted memory of a grander, more powerful past from their original worlds. They were pulled from various planes, bringing with them a chaotic mix of traditions, languages, and, crucially, conflicting claims to ancestral royalty. Their constant infighting and desperate struggle for dominance in Geoctum's less hospitable regions are driven by the distinct philosophies of their leaders.

The Make-Up

Goblins
The most numerous, often serving as the general populace and cannon fodder for the larger tribes. They have a chaotic, opportunistic view of royalty, often worshipping any goblin strong enough to claim a "crown" (often literally, a rusty piece of metal or a bone headdress).

Hobgoblins
The most organized and militaristic of the Ragged Crowns. They cling to stricter hierarchies and military discipline, though even their discipline is often fractured by conflicting loyalties and ancestral claims. Their "Blood Kings" are typically the strongest warlords.

Bugbears
The most brutish, often serving as elite shock troops or personal guards for the Blood Kings. They value stealth and strength, and their leaders are the fiercest and most terrifying.

Ogres
Rare hulking giants notorious for their quick tempers. When its rage is incited, an ogre lashes out in a frustrated tantrum until it runs out of objects or creatures to smash.

Royalty

Every significant goblinoid leader declares themselves a Blood King or Blood Queen. The titles are grand, but the reality is often squalid. Their "thrones" might be piles of salvaged junk, and their "palaces" leaky caves. They obsess over crude crowns or sceptres, often made from scavenged metal, bones, or crudely cut stone, believing these items imbue them with legitimate authority. These items are often fiercely guarded and are objects of intense ritualistic significance. Tribes pledge fealty to their Blood King, often through crude, violent rituals. However, this fealty is notoriously fickle, always open to challenge by a stronger, more ambitious rival.

While many pay lip service to Maglubiyet, Hruggek, and Kurtulmak, some tribes might still revere forgotten deities, or have unique interpretations of the existing gods. This further fragments their religious practices and internal unity.

Tribes

The Iron Legion

A mix of Hobgoblins (often forming the core of the disciplined warriors), Goblins (as foot soldiers and laborers), and Bugbears (as shock troops and enforcers). Crucially, the Iron Legion also boasts a significant number of Ogres in its ranks, serving as brutal, powerful heavy infantry and siege breakers.

This tribe is led by a Blood Queen who is fiercely dedicated to recreating a semblance of the highly militarized, disciplined order of her ancestors' lost hobgoblin empire. They are more organized and strategically minded than other goblinoids, valuing strict drills, tactical formations, and a clear chain of command. They are constantly training, even when resources are scarce, and their camps are surprisingly well-ordered. They view other goblinoid tribes as undisciplined rabble. Their inclusion of Ogres demonstrates their ambition and willingness to incorporate brute force into their disciplined ranks.

The Iron Legion is a highly organized and ruthlessly efficient goblinoid warband that stands in stark contrast to the other chaotic tribes of the Ragged Crowns. Comprised of a disciplined hobgoblin core, swarms of goblin foot soldiers, brutal bugbear shock troops, and a formidable contingent of ogre siege-breakers, the Legion operates less like a rambling horde and more like the remnant of a fallen army. They are often found in defensible, desolate plains, turning ancient ruins or natural rock formations into well-ordered, fortified encampments where the clang of training drills echoes day and night.

The Legion is driven by a singular, ambitious vision: to conquer and assimilate all other goblinoid tribes, forging them into a single, unstoppable legion capable of carving out a new empire. They believe that the scattered, chaotic state of the Ragged Crowns is a disgrace to the memory of their ancestors' glorious (and likely apocryphal) empire. Their goal is not mere survival or plunder, but true conquest and the restoration of a lost martial glory. They view other tribes not as kin, but as undisciplined rabble to be broken and reforged in the fires of their iron discipline.

They are often found trying to establish a fortified encampment in a defensible, rocky plain or a desolate, windswept plateau. They might attempt to repurpose ancient, abandoned structures left behind from the Dark Age.

Blood Queen Vozraz "Iron-Gaze"

A stern and unyielding leader with a scarred face and eyes that burn with cold discipline. She rules through strict adherence to a forgotten martial code and brutal efficiency. She dreams of forging all the Ragged Crowns into a single, unstoppable legion under her command, believing it is the only way for goblinoids to reclaim their "rightful" place. Vozraz is a Hobgoblin in her early 50s.

Settlements

Capital Fortress: Karaz-Voz (The Iron Peak)
Carved into the base of a lesser peak in the Dragon Spine foothills, Karaz-Voz is a formidable fortress-camp. Taking a page from the Dwarves of The Stoneheart Empire, the Iron Legion has excavated great halls and armouries into the mountainside, their entrances guarded by disciplined hobgoblin phalanxes and hulking ogres. The air rings with the sound of training drills and forges. It is from here that Blood Queen Vozraz directs her campaign to unite the goblinoids.

Training Grounds: The Grindstone Plains
A desolate, windswept plateau at the foot of the mountains. This flat, open space is where the Iron Legion conducts its relentless drills, practicing tactical formations and siege warfare. The ground is scarred by practice trenches and shattered training dummies.

The Slag-Mines
An old, abandoned mine repurposed by the Legion to extract low-grade iron ore. Overseen by a ruthless hobgoblin Taskmaster, goblin laborers and slaves toil endlessly to provide the raw materials for the Legion's weapons and armour, fuelling Vozraz's war machine.

Black-Iron Warrens
A series of fortified caves in the smaller mountain ranges near the Stoneheart Empire's borders. This is the Legion's primary armoury. They use the caves to smelt "black steel", a crude but effective metal used to equip their Ogres and heavy infantry.

The Gallow-Glass Ruins
A shattered mirror-glass palace "pulled" from a high-magic plane, now half-buried in the arid plains near Sourock. A site of religious significance. The Blood Kings of the Legion believe the reflections in the glass show glimpses of their ancestral royalty, fuelling their delusions of grandeur and their desire to reclaim their lost glory.

The Skull-Throne Kingdom

A mix of Bugbears (often forming the elite guard and enforcers), Hobgoblins (as administrators and military officers), and Goblins (as servants and common subjects).

This tribe embodies a more royal and organized aspect of goblinoid society, clinging to a distorted memory of a past where goblinoids were rulers, not just brutes. Their leader holds court, demands elaborate (if crude) rituals, and attempts to establish a semblance of a kingdom. They value personal strength, but also a twisted form of loyalty and a brutal sense of nobility. They often use goblins as servants and foot soldiers, treating them as lesser subjects. Their efforts to repair and occupy old fortresses demonstrate their ambition for permanence and greater influence.

Typically found in large, naturally defensible cave systems, deep ravines, or even the ruins of a fortress that they have claimed and crudely ennobled. They also command a number of disused fortresses (often Mehdian, or remnants of other structures) that they are actively, if crudely, trying to repair and make habitable. These serve as their regional outposts and symbols of their expanding kingdom.

Driven by a distorted and violent interpretation of a past where goblinoids were rulers, the Skull-Throne Kingdom seeks legitimacy through conquest. Their goal is to establish a recognized and feared kingdom, complete with subjects, territory, and a royal court. They are obsessed with demanding tribute, not just raiding for loot, from weaker tribes and even isolated surface-dweller settlements. Every piece of treasure collected, every fortress occupied, and every knee bent in fear is, in their eyes, another step toward restoring their "rightful" place as kings of the wastes.

Blood King Vorlag "Skull-Crown”

A massive, imposing bugbear, adorned with crude, but elaborate, bone and metal ornaments, including a crown fashioned from a monster's skull. He is cunning and terrifying, ruling through fear and displays of overwhelming strength. He is obsessed with collecting "tributes" and "treasures" to expand his "kingdom," and constantly seeks to subjugate weaker tribes. He might even try to demand tribute from isolated Oldstone Alliance towns, testing their resolve.

Settlements

Capital: The Bone-Court of Vorlag (Formerly Castle Vayner)
Located in the rocky, southern hills of the Mehdian Heartland, overlooking a key trade route between Valoria and Sourock. Once a strategic castle of the Old Empire, its proud towers are now festooned with crude banners of stitched hides and sharpened bones. The great hall is where Blood King Vorlag holds his terrifying court from atop his Skull-Throne, surrounded by his bugbear elite guard. The dungeons have been repurposed as pens for goblin servants and cells for captured "tribute”.

Regional Outpost: The Rust-Spire (Formerly The Silver-Watch Outpost)
A tall, isolated watchtower in the eastern Mehdian plains. Its stone walls are now stained with rust and smoke. It serves as a regional base for a hobgoblin "lord" loyal to Vorlag, who uses it to launch tribute demands against nearby villages and harass caravans traveling towards Eastway.

Regional Outpost: Fort Grok (Formerly Fort Gyllan)
A crumbling fortress near the edge of the Whispering Woods. It is constantly besieged by monstrous forest creatures, forcing its hobgoblin commander to be particularly vicious and resourceful. This constant fighting makes the garrison one of the most battle-hardened in the entire kingdom.

Dead-Oak Hollow
Situated on the fringes of the Wildfang Thicket, this settlement consists of fortified tree-forts and earthen ramparts. A recruitment and training center. The Legion brings newcomers here to break their spirits and force them into the ranks.

The Gutter-Scourge Tribe

A volatile mix of Goblins (as the most numerous raiders), Hobgoblins (as more organized skirmishers), and Bugbears (as the brutal spearhead of their raids).

This tribe represents the more traditional, chaotic, and opportunistic goblinoid way of life. They are primarily raiders and pillagers, striking swiftly and disappearing back into their hidden lairs. They value cunning, numbers, and the acquisition of easy loot. Their society is loose and anarchic, with leadership often shifting based on who can lead the most successful raid, though their current leader is a formidable Bugbear.

They are highly adaptable, found infesting abandoned mines, forgotten sewers of old ruins, dense thickets, or any place offering quick concealment and easy access to raiding targets. The tribe is driven by the most basic and immediate of goblinoid desires: plunder, food, and the thrill of a successful hunt. They have no grand strategy for conquest or dreams of a restored empire. Their existence is a simple, brutal cycle of identifying a weak target, overwhelming it with a wave of violence, and hauling away anything that isn't nailed down. Leadership is fluid and based entirely on who can promise and deliver the most loot, making their society an anarchic and unstable alliance of greedy individuals.

Blood Chief Krag "Greedy-Fingers”

A cunning and particularly vicious bugbear, renowned for his ability to find weak points in defenses and his insatiable desire for plunder. He leads by example in raids, often being the first to rush into danger (if the loot is good). He has little interest in grand empires or noble titles, only in the immediate spoils of war and the thrill of the hunt. His brute strength makes him a terrifying figurehead for his chaotic tribe.

Settlements

The Scab-Warren
An abandoned mine network not far from the trade route connecting Crossroads Haven and Nestport. The tunnels are a maze of traps, garbage heaps, and goblin dens where Blood Chief Krag hoards his personal loot. Its proximity to the trade route makes it the perfect staging ground for ambushing merchant caravans.

The Sump
This is a major secondary lair for the tribe. It is located within the forgotten sewer system of a ruined city from the Old Empire, situated somewhere between the Mehdian Republic and Vanalir territory. Its labyrinthine nature allows the Legion to emerge unexpectedly, raid nearby settlements, and vanish back into the subterranean tunnels.

The Blighted Fang Tribe

Leader: Witch-Doctor Zik'krik (Goblin, Male)
A hunched and unnervingly clever goblin shaman who leads his tribe by concocting potent poisons and communing with dark, foul spirits of the earth.

The Pox-Water Fen
Deep within the most fetid and stagnant part of the Wildfang Thicket, this tribe makes its home in a series of crude huts built on stilts above bubbling, poison-tainted water. Witch-Doctor Zik'krik chose this location for its abundance of toxic fungi and venomous insects, which he uses in his dark rituals and potent poisons. The air here is heavy with the smell of decay and strange, chemical-like floral scents.

The Rust-Eater Clan

Leader: Forge-Mistress Grulka (Hobgoblin, Female)
A pragmatic and resourceful hobgoblin who commands her clan's exceptional skill at scavenging and repurposing scrap metal into crude but effective weapons and armour.

The Iron Fields
Situated on the desolate plains bordering the western edge of Mehdian territory, the Iron Fields are a vast, ancient battlefield littered with the rusting husks of war machines from a forgotten, civilization. Forge-Mistress Grulka and her clan have established a sprawling workshop-camp here, using a salvaged crane to haul massive pieces of scrap metal, which they melt down and re-forge into crude but effective gear

The Mire-Stalker Mob

Leader: Grol "Silent-Gut" (Bugbear, Male)
A lean and patient bugbear who has perfected the art of the swamp ambush, leading his mob on silent hunts through bogs and marshes that others deem impassable.

The Gloom-Marsh
This tribe inhabits a treacherous, mist-shrouded swamp on the southern edge of the Whispering Woods. Grol "Silent-Gut" leads his bugbears from a network of hidden, semi-submerged caves. They use their knowledge of the marsh's secret paths and quicksand pits to launch devastatingly effective ambushes on any who stray too far from the established roads.

The Shattered-Shield Gang

Leader: Taskmaster Vogg (Hobgoblin, Male)
A scarred and grizzled hobgoblin veteran who drills his small, elite gang in the use of shields and spears, making them a surprisingly formidable defensive force.

The Broken Pass
This small but disciplined tribe under Taskmaster Vogg controls a narrow, strategic pass through the rocky foothills west of Ironriver Crossing. They have fortified the pass with a crude but effective wall of sharpened stakes and shattered shields scavenged from past skirmishes. They extort a "toll" from any smaller travelers or merchants who lack a proper military escort.

The Tunnel-Gnawer Swarm

Leader: Matron Grizzel (Goblin, Female)
An ancient and surprisingly large goblin matriarch who rules a vast warren of tunnels, using overwhelming numbers and her knowledge of the underground to swarm her enemies.
The Gnaw-Holes
Located in the desolate, rocky hills that border the northern Ash Wastes, this vast tribe lives in a sprawling network of natural tunnels and hand-dug warrens. Matron Grizzel rules from the deepest cavern, directing her swarm to emerge onto the plains at night to scavenge for food and drag unsuspecting prey back into their labyrinthine home.

The Grim-Snout Band

Leader: Thunk "One-Eye" (Bugbear, Male)
A famously stubborn and bad-tempered bugbear who leads his band by being the strongest and meanest of the lot, specializing in straightforward, brutal assaults.
The Slag Pit
Thunk "One-Eye" and his band of brutish bugbears have made their camp in a wide, barren box canyon in the heart of the Ash Wastes. They have no permanent structures, only a collection of crude lean-tos and a massive bonfire pit where they roast any creatures they manage to hunt down. Their straightforward, aggressive nature often brings them into bloody conflict with the Cinder-Scourge Clan of Dragonborn over territory and kills.
